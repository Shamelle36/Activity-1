How Republic Act 10630 Works?


1. Apprehension and Investigation:

  • If a child below 15 years old is suspected of an offense, the law emphasizes immediate release to parents/guardians unless deemed necessary for their protection or investigation.
  • Social workers and police must prioritize diversionary measures like mediation, family counselling, and community services.
  • If an official investigation is necessary, it must be conducted with the child's best interests in mind, including access to legal counsel and protection from self-incrimination.

    • 2. Diversion and Intervention:

  • For children below 15, the courts prioritize diversion programs through the Local Social Welfare and Development Office (LSWDO) or accredited NGOs.
  • These programs may involve counseling, education, community service, and restorative justice measures.
  • Only in exceptional cases, if the child exhibits "discernment" and the offense is serious, may the court proceed with formal proceedings.

    • 3. Judicial Proceedings for Children 15-18 Years Old:

  • For children aged 15-18, RA 10630 presumes they lack discernment, but this can be rebutted in court.
  • If discernment is not proven, the court prioritizes diversion programs similar to those for younger children.
  • If discernment is proven, or the offense is serious, the child may be subject to proceedings in special "Family Courts" or, in some cases, regular courts with dedicated juvenile facilities.

    • 4. Detention and Rehabilitation:

  • Detention for CICLs must always be a last resort and separate from adult offenders.
  • Bahay Pag-asa facilities provide short-term residential care and rehabilitation programs focused on education, skills training, and reintegration into society.
  • Emphasis is placed on restorative justice and addressing the root causes of the child's behavior.

    • 5. Reintegration and Aftercare:

  • After completing programs or serving sentences, CICLs receive support services to facilitate their reintegration into society.
  • This may include education, employment assistance, and community support networks.
